We're Going to MARS!


About Nick


I grew up a few miles from the NASA AMES research center in Mountain View, CA. As a kid I got to take a tour of the facility and have been interesed in rockets ever since.

When SpaceX was first created, I was in awe. A private company attempting to do what traditionally has only been done by a select few goverment agencies is either crazy or awesome - likely both.

In the heart of the 2008 recession, out of money, and following three failed launches, the history of the company hinged on the success of their fourth rocket. And against large odds, the rocket made it into low earth orbit. SpaceX survived.

Since then I've followed SpaceX closely. They've been able to re-use booster rockets by landing them on land and DRONE BARGEs. Their rockets have gotten cheaper and if the schedule holds, they will take their first humans to the ISS in 2019.

In 2018 I was able to see the first launch of the Falcon Heavy. When I saw two of the boosters land simultaneously close to where the rocket took off I know one thing:

Mars is in our future!
